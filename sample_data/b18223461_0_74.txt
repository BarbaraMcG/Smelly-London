Under the Public Health (Ophthalmia Neonatorum) Regulations, 1926, the duty of notifying a case of Ophthalmia Neonatorum is placed solely upon the medical practitioner in attendance. This disease is defined as a purulent discharge from the eyes of an infant, commencing within twenty-one days from the date of its birth.
